### Founder's Associate | PAVE Space SA
At PAVE Space, a 25-person startup working on last-mile satellite delivery — moving multi-ton payloads from LEO to any orbit within 24 hours — I got my first taste of what it means to work in a fast-paced, high-ambition environment in a professional setting. Working closely with Julie, the CEO, I learned what early-stage company building really looks like: from writing monthly investor updates and supporting grant applications, to conducting market and competitor research, to building and structuring the investor data room — refining pitch decks, business plans, and go-to-market materials — all in preparation for a seed round that subsequently closed at $40M. Being surrounded by 25 exceptionally talented people made it an environment where I learned a tremendous amount in a very short time.

### Prototyping Coach | EPFL-SPOT
*Student Prototyping and Outreach Tank · Sep 2024 – Jun 2025*

As a Prototyping Coach at EPFL-SPOT, I coached hundreds of students through the full prototyping journey, from brainstorming and strategy all the way to hands-on manufacturing. What I enjoyed most was the variety: every student came with a different project and different challenges, and my role was to guide them on everything from choosing the right fabrication method to actually using the machines, whether that was 3D printing, laser cutting, conventional machining, soldering, or more. Alongside coaching, I maintained and assembled the workshop's 3D printers to keep everything accessible and operational throughout the year.

### Internship in Mechanism Design and Production | Instant-Lab, EPFL
*Jul 2024 – Aug 2024*

My internship at Instant-Lab was my first experience in a research lab environment. The project focused on designing and optimizing a flexure-guided angular inverter: a precision mechanism that inverts an input angle with minimal parasitic motion. Starting from analytical dimensioning, I designed the mechanism in CAD, ran FEM simulations in COMSOL, fabricated laser-cut prototypes, and conducted experimental validation using a camera-based measurement setup. The iterative process of going from theory to simulation to physical testing was deeply satisfying, and seeing the optimized design achieve an ~83% reduction in angular error compared to the baseline made it all the more rewarding.

### Manufacturing Trainee | ETML
*Technical School of Lausanne · Jul 2023 – Aug 2023*

At ETML, I spent a summer on the workshop floor manufacturing and assembling mechanical components where I applied turning, milling, drilling, and welding techniques from technical drawings. More than the machining itself, what stuck with me was developing a much deeper appreciation for 2D drawings and tolerances: understanding firsthand the effort and cost behind every design choice made me a more thoughtful engineer when I returned to CAD and design work.
